What are HTML entities? 
 
 HTML entities are special codes used to display characters that have special meanings in HTML, such as <, >, and &. They are used to represent characters that cannot be typed directly into HTML or are reserved characters in HTML.
 
Five Commonly Used HTML Entities:

<

 Entity Name: &lt;
 Character: <
 Description: Less-than sign

>

 Entity Name: &gt;
 Character: >
 Description: Greater-than sign

&

 Entity Name: &amp;
 Character: &
 Description: Ampersand

"

 Entity Name: &quot;
 Character: "
 Description: Double quotation mark

'

 Entity Name: &apos;
 Character: '
 Description: Apostrophe (single quotation mark)
